## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

### 1. Barre Chords

Look for the ğŸ”’ icon - these chords use barres.

### 2. Capo Frets

When capo is shown (green highlight), place capo at that fret first.
Then play the diagram from fret 1.

### 3. Finger Numbers

- 1 = Index finger
- 2 = Middle finger
- 3 = Ring finger
- 4 = Pinky finger

### 4. Base Fret

The starting fret shown in chord info.
First "fret line" on diagram is always this fret.

### 5. Multiple Voicings

Each chord shows 2-4 different fingering options.
Try all to find which feels most comfortable.

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What do the finger numbers mean?

A: 1=Index, 2=Middle, 3=Ring, 4=Pinky. 0 or X = Open/Muted.

### Q: Why are some chords shown with muted strings?

A: You don't need to play every string. Muted strings give cleaner tone.

### Q: What does "capo" mean?

A: A device that holds down all strings at the same fret. See "Capo" indicator.

### Q: Can I print these chords?

A: Use your browser's Print function (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P).

### Q: How many chords are in the dictionary?

A: 1000+ fingering positions across all 12 keys.

### Q: Can I download this offline?

A: Not yet - this is planned for future versions.

### Q: How accurate are these fingerings?

A: Based on standard guitar playing techniques and professional chord charts.

### Q: Are there other instruments?

A: Currently guitar only. Cavaquinho and ukulele coming soon!
